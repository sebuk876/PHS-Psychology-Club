Who We Are

More Than a Club — A Community

The PHS Psychology Club was founded in 2025 by students who believed that understanding the human mind should be accessible to everyone — not just future therapists or scientists.

We explore everything from the science of habits and decision-making to the complexities of identity, mental health, and human connection. Whether you're considering a career in psychology or simply want to better understand yourself and others, you belong here.

We meet every Monday in Room G207, and every session is designed to be engaging, thought-provoking, and welcoming to all.

What We Cover

Weekly Curriculum

Week 1

Introduction to Psychology

An overview of what psychology is, its history, and why understanding the mind matters in everyday life.

Week 2

The Science of Habits

Exploring how habits form, why they're hard to break, and the neurological loops that drive our behavior.

Week 3

Mental Health Awareness

Understanding anxiety, depression, and stress — breaking stigma and learning how to support those around us.

Week 4

Famous Psychological Experiments

A deep dive into Milgram, Stanford Prison, and Pavlov — what they taught us and the ethics behind them.

Week 5

Social Psychology & Influence

How groups shape our beliefs, the psychology of conformity, and why we make the social decisions we do.

Week 6

Cognitive Biases & Decision Making

Why our brains take shortcuts, how biases affect judgment, and techniques to think more clearly.

Week 7

Personality & Identity

Exploring personality theories, the Big Five model, and how our sense of self develops over time.

Week 8

Emotional Intelligence

What EQ is, why it matters more than IQ in many contexts, and how to develop it in daily life.

Week 9

Psychology of Motivation

Maslow's hierarchy, intrinsic vs extrinsic motivation, and what actually drives human achievement.
